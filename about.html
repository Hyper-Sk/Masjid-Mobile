About masjid e miskeen shah

Miskeen Shah Masjid serves as the central hub for the Muslim community in the area, acting as both a spiritual center and a place of gathering. It stands as a beacon of faith, unity, and community for all Muslims, regardless of their background or level of practice. We are blessed to have a thriving and ever-growing community that surrounds the Masjid, participating wholeheartedly in the wide range of activities and programs it offers on a daily basis. These activities are designed to cater to people of all ages and walks of life, fostering a sense of belonging and purpose among those who attend.

Whether you are a new Muslim, someone curious about Islam, or a lifelong follower of the faith, we warmly invite you to visit the Masjid. It is a welcoming space where you can connect with others, seek spiritual guidance, and participate in various educational and social programs. The Masjid offers an opportunity for individuals to deepen their understanding of Islam, strengthen their faith, and build meaningful relationships within the community. We encourage everyone to come and experience the sense of unity and spiritual fulfillment that Miskeen Shah Masjid provides.

Rules to be followed:

  1. Maintain Cleanliness:
    • Remove your shoes before entering the prayer area.
    • Ensure your clothes and body are clean, following the Islamic guidelines of purity.
    • Dispose of litter in the designated bins to keep the Masjid clean and tidy.
  2. Observe Silence and Respect:
    • Refrain from talking or making unnecessary noise inside the Masjid, especially during prayers or sermons.
    • Turn off or silence your mobile phones to avoid disturbances.
  3. Respect Prayer Times:
    • Arrive on time for prayers and avoid unnecessary movement during Salah (prayer).
    • Do not cross in front of someone praying, as this is considered disrespectful.
  4. Appropriate Dress Code:
    • Dress modestly and respectfully, ensuring clothing covers the body according to Islamic guidelines.
    • Men should cover from navel to knees; women should cover their hair and wear loose-fitting clothing.
  5. Supervise Children:
    • Children are welcome but must be supervised at all times to ensure they do not disturb prayers or others.
    • Encourage children to participate respectfully and teach them the importance of the Masjidâ€™s sanctity.
  6. Use the Facilities Responsibly:
    • Use the ablution (wudu) area with care and avoid wasting water.
    • Keep the bathrooms clean and leave them as you would like to find them.
  7. Respect for Everyone:
    • Treat fellow worshippers, visitors, and the Masjid staff with kindness and respect, regardless of their background.
    • Avoid arguments, confrontations, or gossip within the Masjid premises.
  8. No Unauthorized Activities:
    • Do not use the Masjid for personal business, loud social gatherings, or unrelated activities without prior approval from the administration.
    • Avoid arguments, confrontations, or gossip within the Masjid premises.Posting notices, collecting funds, or distributing materials must be approved by the Masjid management.
  9. Adhere to Masjid Guidelines:
    • Follow any additional rules or instructions set by the Masjid management for special events, Jummah (Friday prayers), or community programs.
    • Cooperate with volunteers and staff who work to maintain the sanctity and organization of the Masjid.
  10. Dawah and Outreach:
    • Non-Muslims are welcome to visit the Masjid; however, they should dress modestly and observe the general rules of respect.
    • Any questions or concerns about Islam can be directed to the designated individuals within the Masjid.
  11. Respect for the Quran:
    • Handle copies of the Quran with respect and avoid placing them on the floor.
    • Keep the Quran in its designated area when not in use.
